Trees and Bushes

About:

In order to create a tree or bush in Blender, you should start by entering edit mode (press tab) and selecting the default cube. Then press M and select merge at center. This will give you one tiny point at the origin. Then toggle to an orthographic view. The easiest way to do this is by scrolling around your cube and pressing option to snap to an orthographic view. Next, use E to extrude from this point outward in different directions. This is how you will construct a wire frame for your tree or bush. Continue to extrude until you have created the trunk and branches in a way that is to your liking. Now move out of orthographic view and use G to grab different points on the frame. Add variation so that your tree or bush is not flat. Then click on the wrench and then select Add Modifier. Select the Skin modifier. Now, press Z and change to Wireframe. Select different vertices and use control + A to scale down the size in several places until you have a more desirable shape. Now, click the green triangle, then Normals, then select Auto Smooth. Choose Shade Smooth. You now need to work on the leaves for the tree or bush. Add an Ico Sphere mesh. Select different vertices of the Ico Sphere mesh and use G to grab at different points and deform the mesh. Once you have a desirable shape, you can move the deformed Ico Sphere mesh so that it rests on the branches of yourr tree or bush. Use the same process to create the rest of your leaves.
